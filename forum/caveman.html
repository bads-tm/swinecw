Greetings to you all.

I am [SR]Caveman, the new Community Manager for Stormregion.

For starters, in case any of you are curious about the origins of my handle, have a look here:

http://www.pureimagination.co.uk/captaincaveman/

Yep, that's me...

I should make it clear that I ain't that hairy. It probably isn't a good match for my personailty either but...

It sums up my job description rather nicely and here is why:

During my interview, the big boss here made one thing clear: My #1 responsibility is to torment the development team endlessly on behalf of the community.

So, somehow the image of me bonking them over the head with a large club came to mind. Add that in with a rank of Captain and suddenly here I was dreaming of this fine furry fellow from my childhood. So Captain Caveman it is, "Cap'n" or "Caveman" for short.

Name: Caveman
Rank: Captain
Serial Number: 1-000-000-B-C


Now that I have set a tone of general silliness (thank a recent Monty Python marathon for that) let's get down to business...


In case all that went right over your head, I am here to work with you guys and gals on making Stormregion games the best they can be. What that means in realistic terms is that I would like to hear all of your opinions on everyting relating to WWII, RTS strategy games and specifically Rush for Berlin and The Panzers series. The way this will work is that if you have ideas, suggestions and questions than we can discuss them on the boards and I will take these issues to the development team. However, we have to be clear right from the start that what can be done (within reason and with resources available to do so) will be done. In others words, no promises except that we will do our best.

The folks here at Stormregion are very intersted in what you have to say so let's hear it!!!

Thanks for the welcome.

I should start straight off the bat by letting you guys know I am not an "I dont know" kind of guy. I think you will agree that its a good thing that this is the case especially because I just got here and I am still getting myself up to speed on all the info floating around here. So if I was that "I dont know" kind of guy, you would see that in 80-90% of the threads here.

What I am all about is getting definitive answers to the questions that all of you raise. Thats the way I want it to be and I have already started with the 4d and pak file issue.

Now that I have covered that and you know my thoughts on this issue, there is something you guys are going to have to get used to as well:

1) There are a couple of issues that do not have definitive answers. Some require discussion, some require a let's wait and see how "x" turns out before we decide about "y" and some require consultation with numerous parties. I hate saying "maybe" and I also hate saying "no" unless I'm certain and hate saying "yes" even more unless I'm certain. Misinformation is a bit of a pet peeve for me. Unfortunately, even saying "yes" can end up as a "no" but I like to get as close to a definitive answer as possible.

2) A lot of the information that comes out to the community from a game like RfB appears in measured doses. Telling everybody everything about the game 9 months before it even comes out is a piss poor way to get people excited and interested in your game. Instead, you need something along the lines of: we are going to talk about game feature A at the beginning of April, feature B in the middle of April, feature C at the end of April and feature D in mid-May. So if you get somebody asking about feature D in mid-April, we are sure as heck are not gonna let the cat out of the bag before its time. Ya dig?

Those are just two examples of when you wont see me shooting straight from the hip. I am sure there will be other situations where a definitive answer is not appropriate either. One thing I can assure you of is, if there isnt a defnitive answer forthcoming, there is a damn good reason for that.

That's the end of my rootin' tootin' shoot em up cowboy routine. I know "Ya dig" is more Shaft than John Wayne but sometimes my fingers simply rebel against my brain and that's the result.
